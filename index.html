Tribute to Vasil Levski

Vasil Ivanov Kunchev, known as Vasil Levski,was born on July 6 (July 18, new style) 1837 in Karlovo. He was a Bulgarian revolutionary and national hero. He was an ideologist and organizer of the Bulgarian national revolution, founder of the Internal Revolutionary Organization (IRO). He is also known as the Apostle of Freedom, for organizing and developing a revolutionary network to liberate Bulgaria from Ottoman rule. He traveled the country and created private revolutionary committees to prepare a general revolution. His dream was a pure and holy republic in which everyone would have equal rights, regardless of their nationality and religion.

Timeline

  • 1837: Born in Karlovo, Bulgaria.
  • 1858: Became a monk under the name Ignatius.
  • 1862: Joined the First Bulgarian Legion in Belgrade.
  • 1869: Founded the Internal Revolutionary Organization (IRO).
  • 1872: Captured by Ottoman authorities.
  • 1873: Executed in Sofia on February 18.

Famous Quote

“If I win, I win for the whole people – if I lose, I lose only myself.” – Vasil Levski
